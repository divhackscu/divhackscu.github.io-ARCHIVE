about divhacks
DivHacks is Columbia's first and only student-run diversity hackathon with an objective to create an empowering and inspirational space for underrepresented students in entrepreneurship and technology. For too long, the technology sector has prioritized the voices of the privileged at the expense of diverse peoples and points of view. DivHacks introduces a space for disenfranchised communities to create technologies that reflect our diverse backgrounds and narratives.
theme
The theme for DivHacks (Fall 2019) is education for all. Promoting equal access to educational resources and supplies is an essential step to increasing diversity in the workplace and our society as a whole. Your objective is to use your creativity, experiences, and skills in technology to create a hack that will address a problem in our society centered around the theme of equal opportunity to education.

faq

Who can attend DivHacks?

DivHacks is open to everyone, regardless of their gender or race. Students from other schools are welcome as well; please be sure to indicate your school on the application form.

Do I need prior experience?

Not at all! DivHacks welcomes people of all skill levels and that includes those who are interested in learning. Throughout the hackathon, there will be workshops aimed towards beginners in different areas including workshops for those with no prior programming experience at all.

What do I need to bring?

Be sure to bring an ID, a computer, charger, any other gear you might need, and yourself! Food will be served throughout the day.

Do I need a team to sign up?

You do not need to have a team or idea in mind before signing up for DivHacks! We encourage attendees to form groups and brainstorm at the beginning of the event.
